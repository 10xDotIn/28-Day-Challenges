# Product Photography Checklist
### Score 8/10 or Above — Every Time

*Based on computer vision analysis of 18 Amazon and eBay headphone listings.*
*Each rule maps directly to a signal the analyzer scores.*

---

## The Short Version

> White background. Sharp focus. Good lighting. Product fills the frame.
> No text. No shadows. High resolution. That's it.

---

## Before You Shoot

### Camera & Equipment

- [ ] Use a camera or phone capable of at least **500 × 500px** output (modern smartphones are fine)
- [ ] Clean your lens — a smudge kills sharpness across the whole image
- [ ] Shoot in the highest resolution your device offers; you can always downsize, never upsize
- [ ] Turn off digital zoom — move your feet instead

### The Background

- [ ] Use a **pure white background** — a $10 white poster board or foam core works perfectly
- [ ] The background must be **bright enough to read as white in the corner pixels** (RGB > 240)
  - Tip: hold the board in front of a window or two lights aimed at the background, not the product
- [ ] **No gradients, textures, wood, fabric, or desert landscapes** — these read as "Busy" and cost you points
- [ ] Never use a digitally composited background (AI-generated scenes, stock photo backdrops) — ELA analysis flags these as heavily edited and destroys trust

### Lighting

- [ ] Use **two light sources** — one on each side of the product — to eliminate harsh shadows
- [ ] Natural window light works; just avoid direct sunlight (creates hard shadows)
- [ ] Aim lights at the **background** as well as the product to keep it bright and even
- [ ] **No single overhead light** — this creates the drop shadow underneath that tanks your score
- [ ] Test: take a shot and check the background corners — they should be close to pure white, not gray

---

## Shooting the Product

### Framing & Centering

- [ ] **Center the product** in the frame — not tilted, not pushed to one corner
- [ ] Product should fill **40–80% of the frame** — too small looks lost; too large gets cropped
  - Practical test: the product should have a visible gap of roughly one product-width on all four sides
- [ ] Shoot at a **slight 3/4 angle** (front-left or front-right) — shows depth and dimensionality
- [ ] For earbuds or multi-piece products: arrange all components (earbuds + case) in the frame together

### Focus & Sharpness

- [ ] Tap to focus on the **center of the product**, not the background
- [ ] Use a tripod or prop your phone against something solid — even minor shake causes blur
- [ ] Check the shot at 100% zoom before moving on — if the brand logo isn't crispy, reshoot
- [ ] Target a **Laplacian variance above 80** (Sharp rating) — blurry scores zero for sharpness

### Shadow Control

- [ ] After setting up lights, check for shadows cast **behind or beneath the product**
- [ ] Soft shadows (faint gray near the base) cost **−1 point**; hard shadows cost **−2 points**
- [ ] To eliminate drop shadows:
  - Raise the product off the background (use a small clear acrylic riser or glass shelf)
  - Add a light aimed upward from below to fill the shadow area
  - Or shoot flat-lay (product lying flat, camera pointing straight down) — no drop shadow possible

### What NOT to Add

- [ ] **No text overlays** — no "SALE", no feature callouts, no watermarks, no brand logos added in editing
  - Text overlays reduce engagement score by 1 point and make the image look like an ad, not a product
- [ ] **No banners, badges, or stickers** in the image itself
- [ ] **No props** in the main listing image (lifestyle shots are fine for secondary images)

---

## After You Shoot

### Editing Rules

- [ ] Adjust **brightness and contrast** to ensure the image is well-lit (mean pixel value 60–220)
- [ ] Do **not** over-brighten — if the background blows out to pure white and the product loses detail, pull back
- [ ] A small **levels or curves adjustment** to make the white background truly white is fine
- [ ] **Do not composite** the product onto a new background in Photoshop — ELA detects this
- [ ] Save as **JPEG at 85–95% quality** or **PNG** — do not over-compress

### Final Checklist Before Upload

- [ ] Background corners are white (not gray, not beige)
- [ ] Product is sharp — you can read the brand name clearly
- [ ] No shadows visible behind or beneath the product
- [ ] Product is centered with breathing room on all sides
- [ ] No text, graphics, or watermarks overlaid on the image
- [ ] Image is at least 500 × 500px (1000 × 1000px or higher preferred)
- [ ] Lighting is even — no one side noticeably darker than the other

## Scoring Breakdown

Each rule below maps to a point in the engagement score (max 10):

| Signal | How to Earn It | Points |
|--------|---------------|--------|
| Sharpness | Sharp focus, no motion blur | +2 |
| Contrast | Even lighting creates natural contrast | +2 |
| White background | Pure white, RGB > 240 in corners | +1 |
| Product coverage | Product fills 40–80% of frame | +1 |
| Exposure | Well-lit, not blown out or dark | +1 |
| No text overlay | Nothing printed over the image | +1 |
| Colors pop | Product has visible saturation | +1 |
| High resolution | At least 500 × 500px | +1 |
| **Shadow (Hard)** | Uncontrolled drop shadow | **−2** |
| **Shadow (Soft)** | Faint shadow still visible | **−1** |

**To score 8+: hit all 8 positive signals and avoid hard shadows.**

## One-Minute Version (Print This Out)

```
1. White poster board background
2. Two lights — one each side
3. Product centered, fills ~60% of frame
4. Tap to focus on the product
5. Check for shadows — eliminate them
6. No text added in editing
7. Save high-res, don't over-compress
8. Check it looks sharp on your phone screen before uploading
```

*Follow these 8 steps and you will score 8/10 or above on every shot.*
